Spark

Designing a conceptual app to help people make progress on goals that matter

We all have goals we want to achieve, but life can get in the way - or we can get discouraged. Spark is a conceptual app I designed with two classmates for a UI & Interaction Design course at UC Berkeley Extension. Our vision was to create an inspiring, delightful experience that would help people stay motivated and make consistent progress on their goals through emphasizing small wins.

The Challenge

The inspiration from Spark came from a problem we'd all faced - having goals that were important to us but had fallen by the wayside. We knew we weren't alone; we read that up to 92% of people abandon or postpone their goals. Reasons included competing priorities taking over, feeling discouraged by the complexity of the task ahead, or simply losing interest or motivation.

With Spark, we wanted to design a solution that could solve for the pain points and obstacles people faced in achieving their goals. We also wanted it to be something people wanted to use - we'd heard that tracking apps and tools often felt like 'overhead,' so keeping our solution lightweight, enjoyable, and truly helpful was a priority.

Solution

A key experience within Spark is working on a 'micro-goal' - a task that supports your larger goal, but is small enough so that you can squeeze it into your day - whether you have an hour to spare or just five minutes. Through consistently tackling these micro-goals, people can feel more accomplished, more easily stay on track, and end up well on their way towards achieving their goals.
